About The Conference

Join us for the premier event for African Securities Exchanges

28th ASEA Annual Conference

The ASEA Annual Conference brings together leaders from securities exchanges, market regulators, brokers, investors, and other stakeholders to discuss critical issues affecting African capital markets.

This year's theme, "Adapting to Global Market Shifts: Strategies for Resilience and Growth," explores how African exchanges can navigate global economic challenges and leverage opportunities for sustainable growth.

Rwanda: Africa's Hub of Innovation

Known for its remarkable transformation, Rwanda has emerged as one of Africa's most dynamic economies and a leader in sustainable development. Nicknamed "The Land of a Thousand Hills," Rwanda offers breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural experiences, and a business environment that fosters innovation and growth.

As the host for the 28th ASEA Annual Conference, Rwanda exemplifies the resilience and forward-thinking approach that characterizes modern African capital markets. Kigali, the capital city, combines traditional Rwandan hospitality with world-class infrastructure, making it the perfect destination for this landmark event.

Explore Rwanda

Must-visit attractions while you're attending the conference

Volcanoes National Park

Home to the endangered mountain gorillas and golden monkeys. Experience the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to trek and observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

The park is also known for its stunning volcanic landscapes, offering breathtaking views and hiking opportunities through bamboo forests and open meadows.

Learn More

Lake Kivu

One of Africa's Great Lakes offering beautiful beaches, water sports, and boat tours. Enjoy stunning sunsets and the peaceful atmosphere of this magnificent lake.

The shores of Lake Kivu are dotted with charming towns and beaches where you can relax, swim, or enjoy water activities in a safe, bilharzia-free environment.

Learn More

Kigali Genocide Memorial

A powerful and moving tribute to the victims of the 1994 genocide. The memorial offers important historical context and honors the resilience of the Rwandan people.

This important historical site provides visitors with a profound understanding of Rwanda's past and its remarkable journey toward reconciliation and rebuilding.

Learn More

Nyungwe Forest National Park

One of Africa's oldest rainforests with over 13 primate species, including chimpanzees. Experience the thrilling canopy walkway suspended high above the forest floor.

The park is a biodiversity hotspot with hundreds of bird species, butterflies, and orchids, offering incredible opportunities for nature lovers and photographers.
