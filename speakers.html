Speaker Profile

Idrissa Diop

CEO, Abidjan Stock Exchange

Idrissa Diop has been the Chief Executive Officer of the Abidjan Stock Exchange since 2018. With over 20 years of experience in capital markets, he has led significant reforms that have positioned the exchange as a key player in the West African Economic and Monetary Union (WAEMU) region.

Prior to his current role, Mr. Diop held senior positions at various financial institutions across Africa and Europe. He holds an MBA from Harvard Business School and a Bachelor's degree in Economics from the University of Dakar.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, Mr. Diop will be speaking about regional integration of African capital markets and how exchanges can collaborate to enhance liquidity and market depth.

Session: "Regional Integration: Building Stronger African Capital Markets"

Date: November 26, 2025 | Time: 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM | Venue: Main Hall

Speaker Profile

Izabella Kaminska

Financial Journalist

Izabella Kaminska is a renowned financial journalist with expertise in global markets, financial innovation, and emerging economies. She has written extensively about capital markets in Africa and their potential for growth.

Ms. Kaminska previously worked for the Financial Times where she was the editor of the Alphaville section, covering market developments and financial innovation. She has also contributed to various international publications and is a regular speaker at global financial conferences.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, Ms. Kaminska will moderate a panel discussion on media representation of African capital markets and how improved communication strategies can attract global investors.

Session: "Changing the Narrative: Media and African Capital Markets"

Date: November 27, 2025 | Time: 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM | Venue: Conference Room B

Speaker Profile

Dr. Sarah Alade

Director General, SEC Nigeria

Dr. Sarah Alade serves as the Director General of the Securities and Exchange Commission of Nigeria, bringing over 25 years of experience in financial regulation and capital market development.

She holds a PhD in Economics and has previously worked with the Central Bank of Nigeria and various international financial institutions. Dr. Alade has been instrumental in implementing regulatory reforms that have enhanced investor confidence in Nigerian capital markets.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, Dr. Alade will lead discussions on regulatory harmonization across African markets and investor protection frameworks.

Session: "Regulatory Excellence: Building Trust in African Markets"

Date: November 26, 2025 | Time: 3:00 PM - 4:30 PM | Venue: Main Hall

Speaker Profile

James Mwangi

Chief Technology Officer, NSE

James Mwangi is the Chief Technology Officer at the Nairobi Securities Exchange, leading digital transformation initiatives that have modernized trading and settlement systems across East Africa.

With a background in computer science and financial technology, Mr. Mwangi has spearheaded the implementation of blockchain technology, automated trading systems, and mobile-based investment platforms.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, he will present on the future of fintech in African capital markets and emerging technologies reshaping the investment landscape.

Session: "Digital Revolution: Technology Transforming African Exchanges"

Date: November 27, 2025 | Time: 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM | Venue: Tech Hub

Speaker Profile

Amina Hassan

Managing Partner, Cairo Capital

Amina Hassan is the Managing Partner at Cairo Capital, one of Africa's leading investment firms specializing in cross-border transactions and capital market development.

With over 15 years of experience in investment banking and private equity, Ms. Hassan has facilitated over $2 billion in transactions across African markets. She holds an MBA from Wharton and is a CFA charterholder.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, she will share insights on attracting international investment to African markets and building sustainable investment ecosystems.

Session: "Capital Flows: Attracting Global Investment to Africa"

Date: November 26, 2025 | Time: 4:45 PM - 6:00 PM | Venue: Investment Forum

Speaker Profile

Robert Nyamwasa

CEO, Central Securities Depository

Robert Nyamwasa serves as the Chief Executive Officer of the Central Securities Depository of Rwanda, where he has led the development of modern post-trade infrastructure systems.

His expertise in securities settlement, custody services, and cross-border trading systems has been instrumental in connecting African markets with global financial infrastructure.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, Mr. Nyamwasa will discuss the evolution of post-trade services and the role of CSDs in facilitating regional market integration.

Session: "Post-Trade Infrastructure: Building Connected African Markets"

Date: November 27, 2025 | Time: 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM | Venue: Operations Center

Speaker Profile

Prof. Fatima Al-Rashid

Professor of Finance, American University of Cairo

Professor Fatima Al-Rashid is a distinguished academic and researcher at the American University of Cairo, specializing in African capital markets development and financial systems integration.

Her research has been published in leading academic journals and she has advised multiple African governments on capital market development strategies. She holds a PhD in Finance from London School of Economics.

At the ASEA Conference 2025, Prof. Al-Rashid will present her latest research on sustainable finance frameworks and ESG integration in African markets.

Session: "Sustainable Finance: ESG Integration in African Capital Markets"

Date: November 27, 2025 | Time: 3:45 PM - 5:00 PM | Venue: Research Hall
