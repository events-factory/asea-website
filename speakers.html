Speaker Profile

Frank Mwiti

CEO, Nairobi Securities Exchange

Mr. Frank Mwiti is the CEO of the Nairobi Securities Exchange Plc. Mr. Mwiti is a dynamic and accomplished senior business leader with more than two decades of diverse global and regional professional experience in strategy, governance, business development, Corporate Finance, M&A, investment banking, risk management and performance improvement. He has advised governments, investors, shareholders, boards and CEOs across the UK, US, EU, Russia, Middle East, Asia and Africa.

Prior to his appointment as the CEO of the Nairobi Securities Exchange Plc, he served as a Partner and Eastern Africa Markets Leader at EY, leading Strategy, M&A and Business Development across Kenya, Ethiopia, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, Somalia and South Sudan. He previously held senior executive roles in London (UK) at UBS Investment Bank, Deutsche Bank, EY and PwC.

Mr. Mwiti equally served as CEO and Managing Director at Afrika Kapital in the UK, a firm he founded and in which he was involved in advising investment banks, private equity firms and hedge funds across forex, equities, fixed income, commodities, synthetics and listed derivatives in London, New York, Frankfurt, Singapore, Moscow, and Hong Kong.

Mr. Mwiti holds an Executive Management qualification from MIT (US), a Bachelor of Laws (LL.B) degree from the University of Nairobi and is a member of the Institute of Certified Public Accountants of Kenya (ICPAK).

Speaker Profile

Nandini Sukumar

Chief Executive Officer, World Federation of Exchanges

Nandini Sukumar is the Chief Executive Officer of the World Federation of Exchanges, the global association for exchanges and CCPs. The WFE represents more than 250 exchanges and clearing houses globally, educating stakeholders on the vital role played by market infrastructures in the real economy and as a standard setter, finding the consensus on issues among the global membership. Of our members, 37% are in Asia-Pacific, 44% in EMEA and 19% in the Americas. WFE’s 87 member CCPs and clearing services collectively ensure that risk takers post some $1.3 trillion (equivalent) of resources to back their positions, in the form of initial margin and default fund requirements. WFE exchanges, together with other exchanges feeding into our database, are home to over 51,000 listed companies, and the market capitalisation of these entities is over $110 trillion; around $140 trillion (EOB) in trading annually passes through WFE members (at end 2024). Ms. Sukumar is Vice Chair of IOSCO’s Affiliate Members Consultative Committee and Chair of the AMCC’s Sustainability Taskforce.

Pierre Celestin Rwabukumba

CEO, RSE & President, ASEA

Mr. Pierre Celestin RWABUKUMBA, a Rwandan national, is the current President of the African Securities Exchanges Association (ASEA), Former Chairman of the East African Securities Exchanges Association (EASEA), and Chief Executive Officer of the Rwanda Stock Exchange Ltd. He is also serving on a number of other boards and advisory boards within the financial sector in Rwanda, hospitality, and manufacturing industries where he serves as a non – Executive Director.

Over the past two decades, he has been involved in different investment projects ranging from manufacturing, Real Estate, Poultry and Agro-processing to Energy production. Mr. Rwabukumba started his career in financial services in New York City more than a two decades ago after graduating from the University of Buffalo (UB), New York in the United States with A bachelor's Degree in Economics.

He worked in different professional positions as a stockbroker, in Compliance and Operations, Institutional Investors' Sales and Advisory before he joined the Central Bank of Rwanda in 2004 to help start the Capital Market Development Project in Rwanda. Mr. Rwabukumba is a seasoned negotiator as he has been instrumental in different negotiations on regional integration of the East African Community and the Economic Community of Central African States (ECAS).

He has served on different Capital Market Committees both at the national and the regional level the latest being the High-Level Task Force negotiating the EAC Monetary Union and the EAC Regional Capital Markets Infrastructure (CMI) Steering Committee where he serves as Chair.

Mr. Rwabukumba is an Alumni of a one-year international post-Graduate Advanced Management Program (AMP) for Executives run jointly by Strathmore Business School of Nairobi, the Lagos Business School, Pan- Atlantic University and IESE Business School, the University of Navara in Barcelona, Spain

Peterson Ekow Afedzie

Former Managing Director, Ghana Stock Exchange

BRIEF PROFILE OF MR. PETERSON EKOW AFEDZIE

Mr. Afedzie is the former Managing Director of the Ghana Stock Exchange, a former member of the Council (Board) of the Exchange and a lawyer. Mr. Afedzie worked with the Ghana Stock Exchange since its establishment in 1990 for over 32 years and has over 20 years board experience with various institutions. He served on the Boards of the Central Securities Depository (CSD) Ghana LTD., Ghana Investment and Securities Institute Ltd and Venture Capital Trust Fund. Mr. Afedzie was also a member of the governing committees of Ghana Alternative Market (GAX) and Ghana Fixed Income Market (GFIM). Mr. Afedzie was the Chairman of the West Africa Capital Markets Integration Council and the Co-Chair of the Africa Regional Committee of the International Capital Market Association (ICMA) with sole responsibility over the West African Sub-Committee. He is the former Vice President of the African Securities Exchanges Association (ASEA) and was a member of the Executive Committee of ASEA. He currently serves on the council of the Institute of Directors, Ghana.

He has been associated with many transformational projects at the Ghana Stock Exchange. He was instrumental in molding the GSE and making it relevant to Ghana's financial sector. His zeal to succeed and keep the beacon lit reflected in the planning, coordination and implementation of many transformational projects such as the automation of the trading, clearing and settlement systems, the establishment of the Depository as well as the creation of the Ghana Fixed Income Market (GFIM) and the Ghana Alternative Market (GAX). His diligent drafting of the rules that govern the Exchange cannot be overemphasized. During his tenure as the Managing Director, the GSE was admitted to full membership of the International Capital Market Association as well as the prestigious World Federation of Exchanges. His efficient leadership is further evidenced by the GSE recording its highest net profit after tax in its history under his watch.

He was educated at the University of Ghana, Legon, Ghana where he obtained a master's degree in business administra3on, (MBA) in 1989, a Master of Arts degree (MA) in Economic Policy Management in 2010 and a B.Sc. (Hons) in Agriculture in 1985. He also holds a bachelor's degree in Law (LLB) from Mountcrest University College and a Qualifying Cer3ficate in law from the Ghana School of Law. He is a member of the Ghana Bar Associaon, and a full member of the Chartered Instute of Marke3ng Ghana (CIMG).

Akane Zoukpo Sanankoua

Division Manager, Capital Markets Development, AfDB

Biography (English):

Mrs. Akane Zoukpo Sanankoua is the Division Manager of the Capital Markets Development Division at the African Development Bank Group in charge of private sector and public sector capital markets development transactions across Africa using the range of AfDB instruments. Akane has been instrumental in expanding the Bank's growing partial credit guarantee interventions in support of sustainable financing mobilization for the Bank's clients. Akane joined the Bank in 2014 and has served in several roles within the Capital Markets Development Division, most recently as Chief Investment Officer responsible for leading a broad range of African capital markets development operations and activities.

Akane started her career in investment banking in 2007 at Citigroup, where she worked in the Debt Capital Markets department based in London. At Citi, she provided interest rate and currency risk hedging solutions for European corporates. Akane holds a Master's in Public Administration (MPA) from the Harvard Kennedy School of Government and a Master's of Science (MSc) in Management - specializing in Finance, from ESCP Europe.

Contact: a.zoukpo-sanankoua@afdb.org

Speaker Profile

Bob Karina

Market Builder | Investment Banker | Regional Integration Champion

Professional Background

Bob J. Karina is one of Africa's most respected and active investment bankers, at the forefront of capital markets transformation across the continent. For over 25 years, he has been shaping East Africa's financial architecture - founding and scaling institutions, modernising exchanges, and advising boards, governments, and sovereign agencies on how to mobilise capital for growth.

Current Leadership Roles

As the Founder & Chairman of Faida Investment Bank (Kenya) and Managing Director of Faida Securities Rwanda, both institutions continue to deliver landmark transactions, widen investor participation, and mobilise capital for ambitious issuers across the region. As Immediate Past Chairman of the Rwanda Stock Exchange (2020-2025) and of the Nairobi Securities Exchange (2006-2015), Bob is recognised as a governance leader with a unique cross-border perspective on risk, compliance, and sustainable market development.

Market Transformation Leadership

As a real time change-maker, Bob continues to drive the digitisation of markets, deepen cross-border listings, and strengthen governance frameworks that attract long-term capital into Africa. His influence spans from designing policies that power integration, to advising boards on structuring growth capital, to working with governments on financing critical infrastructure.

Global Recognition & Thought Leadership

As an internationally sought-after thought leader, Bob has twice addressed the AIM Congress in Abu Dhabi, engaging global exchange leaders on Africa's integration and investment agenda; and is also an active member of the Institute of Directors (Kenya) and a Founder Member & Fellow of ICFA, combining boardroom credibility with technical depth.

Education & Vision

Holding an MSc in Corporate Finance from the University of Liverpool, Bob operates at the intersection of vision and execution- actively shaping Africa's next chapter of financial growth. Still at the peak of his career, his mission is clear: to help boards, investors, and governments across Africa design capital markets that finance the continent's transformation.

Personal Philosophy

Beyond the boardroom, Bob is family-centered, married to Dr. Jennifer Karina, with three adult children. He enjoys swimming, golf, and running - pursuits that mirror his philosophy of endurance, consistency, and discipline.
