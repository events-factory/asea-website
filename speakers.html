Frank Mwiti

CEO, Nairobi Securities Exchange

Mr. Frank Mwiti is the CEO of the Nairobi Securities Exchange Plc. Mr. Mwiti is a dynamic and accomplished senior business leader with more than two decades of diverse global and regional professional experience in strategy, governance, business development, Corporate Finance, M&A, investment banking, risk management and performance improvement. He has advised governments, investors, shareholders, boards and CEOs across the UK, US, EU, Russia, Middle East, Asia and Africa.

Prior to his appointment as the CEO of the Nairobi Securities Exchange Plc, he served as a Partner and Eastern Africa Markets Leader at EY, leading Strategy, M&A and Business Development across Kenya, Ethiopia, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, Somalia and South Sudan. He previously held senior executive roles in London (UK) at UBS Investment Bank, Deutsche Bank, EY and PwC.

Mr. Mwiti equally served as CEO and Managing Director at Afrika Kapital in the UK, a firm he founded and in which he was involved in advising investment banks, private equity firms and hedge funds across forex, equities, fixed income, commodities, synthetics and listed derivatives in London, New York, Frankfurt, Singapore, Moscow, and Hong Kong.

Mr. Mwiti holds an Executive Management qualification from MIT (US), a Bachelor of Laws (LL.B) degree from the University of Nairobi and is a member of the Institute of Certified Public Accountants of Kenya (ICPAK).

Speaker Profile

Nandini Sukumar

Chief Executive Officer, World Federation of Exchanges

Nandini Sukumar is the Chief Executive Officer of the World Federation of Exchanges, the global association for exchanges and CCPs. The WFE represents more than 250 exchanges and clearing houses globally, educating stakeholders on the vital role played by market infrastructures in the real economy and as a standard setter, finding the consensus on issues among the global membership. Of our members, 37% are in Asia-Pacific, 44% in EMEA and 19% in the Americas. WFE’s 87 member CCPs and clearing services collectively ensure that risk takers post some $1.3 trillion (equivalent) of resources to back their positions, in the form of initial margin and default fund requirements. WFE exchanges, together with other exchanges feeding into our database, are home to over 51,000 listed companies, and the market capitalisation of these entities is over $110 trillion; around $140 trillion (EOB) in trading annually passes through WFE members (at end 2024). Ms. Sukumar is Vice Chair of IOSCO’s Affiliate Members Consultative Committee and Chair of the AMCC’s Sustainability Taskforce.

Hon. Marlene Street Forrest, OJ, CD, PhD. (Hon.), JP

Managing Director, Jamaica Stock Exchange

Dr. Marlene Street Forrest is the Managing Director of the Jamaica Stock Exchange (JSE) and Director of both its subsidiaries. Her mandate is to continue the process of developing the JSE Group and particularly the Exchange, in an atmosphere of transparency and fairness while utilizing appropriate technology in providing the greatest possible efficiencies to the market.

Bob Karina

Market Builder | Investment Banker | Regional Integration Champion

Professional Background

Bob J. Karina is one of Africa's most respected and active investment bankers, at the forefront of capital markets transformation across the continent. For over 25 years, he has been shaping East Africa's financial architecture - founding and scaling institutions, modernising exchanges, and advising boards, governments, and sovereign agencies on how to mobilise capital for growth.

Current Leadership Roles

As the Founder & Chairman of Faida Investment Bank (Kenya) and Managing Director of Faida Securities Rwanda, both institutions continue to deliver landmark transactions, widen investor participation, and mobilise capital for ambitious issuers across the region. As Immediate Past Chairman of the Rwanda Stock Exchange (2020-2025) and of the Nairobi Securities Exchange (2006-2015), Bob is recognised as a governance leader with a unique cross-border perspective on risk, compliance, and sustainable market development.

Market Transformation Leadership

As a real time change-maker, Bob continues to drive the digitisation of markets, deepen cross-border listings, and strengthen governance frameworks that attract long-term capital into Africa. His influence spans from designing policies that power integration, to advising boards on structuring growth capital, to working with governments on financing critical infrastructure.

Global Recognition & Thought Leadership

As an internationally sought-after thought leader, Bob has twice addressed the AIM Congress in Abu Dhabi, engaging global exchange leaders on Africa's integration and investment agenda; and is also an active member of the Institute of Directors (Kenya) and a Founder Member & Fellow of ICFA, combining boardroom credibility with technical depth.

Education & Vision

Holding an MSc in Corporate Finance from the University of Liverpool, Bob operates at the intersection of vision and execution- actively shaping Africa's next chapter of financial growth. Still at the peak of his career, his mission is clear: to help boards, investors, and governments across Africa design capital markets that finance the continent's transformation.

Personal Philosophy

Beyond the boardroom, Bob is family-centered, married to Dr. Jennifer Karina, with three adult children. He enjoys swimming, golf, and running - pursuits that mirror his philosophy of endurance, consistency, and discipline.

Dr. Islam Abdel Azim Azzam is a distinguished financial regulator and academic with extensive experience in capital markets oversight, insurance regulation, and non-banking financial services. He served as Deputy Chairman of Egypt's Financial Regulatory Authority from January 2021 to August 2025, overseeing capital markets, insurance, and non-banking financial sectors.
